# Rhino Bot Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 19, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Rhino Bot ("the Bot") processes information when it is used in Discord servers for Northeast Esports community management, moderation, verification, modmail, support tickets, suggestions, NE Coins, shop rewards, server utilities, and related Discord server features.

The Bot is operated by the server owner, administrators, and authorized staff for the Discord server where it is installed. The Bot is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by Discord Inc.

## 1. Relationship to Discord

The Bot runs on Discord and depends on Discord APIs, permissions, and user interactions. Your use of Discord is also governed by Discord's own Terms of Service, Privacy Policy, Community Guidelines, Developer Terms of Service, and Developer Policy.

Official Discord policy pages include:

- Discord Terms of Service: https://discord.com/terms
- Discord Privacy Policy: https://discord.com/privacy
- Discord Community Guidelines: https://discord.com/guidelines
- Discord Developer Terms of Service: https://support-dev.discord.com/hc/en-us/articles/8562894815383-Discord-Developer-Terms-of-Service
- Discord Developer Policy: https://support-dev.discord.com/hc/en-us/articles/8563934450327-Discord-Developer-Policy

Discord may separately collect, process, store, and retain data according to Discord's own policies.

## 2. Information the Bot May Process

The Bot may process Discord information that is made available through Discord permissions, server configuration, commands, buttons, modals, direct messages, and normal server activity. This may include:

- Discord user IDs, usernames, display names, avatars, server IDs, channel IDs, role IDs, message IDs, thread IDs, invite codes, and timestamps.
- Message content when needed for enabled features such as modmail, support tickets and their transcripts, no-link checks, AFK replies, message edit or delete logs, moderation context, staff applications, and command handling.
- Attachment names, attachment URLs, and image indicators when messages are logged, relayed, or exported by enabled moderation, server-log, modmail, or ticket features.
- Moderation records such as warnings, timeouts, kicks, bans, unbans, role changes, reasons, moderator IDs, target user IDs, durations, and timestamps.
- Verification events, modmail session metadata, ticket ownership and claim metadata, staff or referee application responses, suggestions, suggestion votes, NE Coins balances and transactions, shop purchases, reward fulfillment records, AFK reasons, custom command prefixes, auto-reaction settings, no-link channel settings, anti-raid events, and server statistics.
- Server activity events such as member joins and leaves, role updates, channel updates, emoji updates, voice state changes, invite create or delete events, message edits, message deletes, and bulk deletes.

The Bot does not ask for Discord passwords, Discord tokens, payment card details, government IDs, health information, or other sensitive information that is not needed for its stated server-management features.

## 3. How Information Is Used

The Bot uses processed information only to provide and maintain its server-management, moderation, safety, support, and community features. These uses may include:

- Applying moderation actions requested by authorized staff.
- Sending moderation, verification, invite, server activity, and anti-raid logs to configured Discord channels.
- Relaying modmail messages between users and server staff.
- Creating private support channels and exporting ticket messages, attachments, embeds, authors, and timestamps into HTML transcripts for participants and authorized staff.
- Reviewing staff or referee applications submitted through Bot modals.
- Assigning verification roles when users complete the verification flow.
- Detecting and blocking links in configured no-link channels.
- Managing AFK notices, command prefixes, auto-reaction rules, QOTD posts, welcome messages, suggestions, NE Coins, shop rewards, and server statistics.
- Troubleshooting errors, abuse, permission issues, rate limits, and operational problems.

The Bot does not sell Discord API data, use it for targeted advertising, disclose it to data brokers, or use message content to train machine learning or AI models.

## 4. Storage and Locations

Depending on configuration, the Bot may store some data in:

- PostgreSQL, when `DATABASE_URL` is configured.
- Local JSON files controlled by the Bot operator, when PostgreSQL is not configured.
- Discord channels, threads, direct messages, forum threads, and audit-style log channels selected by server administrators.
- Hosting, deployment, database, or logging infrastructure used to run the Bot.

Stored data may include moderation logs, AFK statuses, command prefixes, auto-reaction settings, no-link channel settings, staff application submissions, suggestions, suggestion votes, NE Coins balances and transactions, shop items, shop purchases, reward fulfillment records, modmail-related messages, ticket transcripts, and server-management records.

Some information is only kept in memory while the Bot is running, such as temporary interaction state, active modmail sessions, application drafts before submission, cooldowns, and anti-raid state.

## 5. Retention

Retention depends on the feature, server configuration, and operational needs:

- AFK statuses are normally removed when the user sends a message in the server or when the status is otherwise cleared.
- Staff application drafts are temporary before submission, while submitted applications may remain in the configured review channel.
- Moderation logs, server logs, modmail records, ticket transcripts, verification logs, invite logs, and anti-raid records may be kept as long as server staff reasonably need them for moderation, support, safety, audit, abuse prevention, or server-management purposes.
- Auto-reaction, no-link channel, and command-prefix settings may be kept until disabled, reset, deleted, or no longer needed.

Discord may retain messages, logs, attachments, and other content according to Discord's own policies and the settings of the Discord server.

## 6. Sharing and Access

Information processed by the Bot may be visible to:

- Server administrators, moderators, and authorized staff who manage the Bot or review configured log channels.
- Users involved in a feature, such as modmail participants, ticket participants, or users receiving moderation notices.
- Discord, because the Bot operates on Discord.
- Hosting, database, deployment, or infrastructure providers used to operate the Bot.

The Bot operator does not sell user data or share it for unrelated advertising or marketing purposes.

## 7. User Choices and Requests

You can reduce Bot data processing by not interacting with Bot commands, buttons, modals, or direct-message features, and by following the rules of servers where the Bot is installed.

For questions, access requests, correction requests, or deletion requests related to Bot data, contact the administrators or moderators of the Discord server where the Bot is installed. If modmail is enabled, you may contact staff by sending a direct message to the Bot and following the modmail prompt.

Server staff may need your Discord user ID, server name, and relevant context to locate records. Requests may be limited or denied when needed for moderation integrity, safety, abuse prevention, legal compliance, protection of other users, Discord policy compliance, or technical feasibility.

## 8. Security

The Bot operator uses reasonable measures to protect Bot configuration and stored data, including keeping Bot tokens and database credentials out of the public repository. However, no online service, Discord bot, database, or hosting environment can be guaranteed to be completely secure.

If you believe Bot data has been exposed or misused, contact the server administrators or moderators as soon as possible.

## 9. Children's Privacy

The Bot is intended for use on Discord by users who meet Discord's minimum age requirements. The Bot is not directed to children under 13 or to anyone below the minimum age required by law in their location.

## 10. Changes to This Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated from time to time. The updated version will be effective when posted or made available. Continued use of the Bot after updates means the updated policy applies to your use of the Bot.

## 11. Contact

For privacy questions about Rhino Bot, contact the administrators or moderators of the Discord server where Rhino Bot is installed. If modmail is enabled, you may contact staff by sending a direct message to the Bot and following the modmail prompt.
